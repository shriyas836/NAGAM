Stray Animal Management - NAGAM

Nagam is committed to managing stray animals, ensuring their safety, health, and addressing public concerns related to their presence in the city.

The department works towards animal welfare by providing shelter, healthcare, and adopting strategies to control the stray animal population...

The Stray Animal Management department is dedicated to controlling the population of stray animals in the city. This includes capturing, vaccinating, and providing medical care to stray animals, as well as ensuring their safe return to their rightful locations or relocation to shelters.

The department focuses on animal welfare by ensuring that stray animals are not left in hazardous conditions. They are vaccinated, treated for injuries or diseases, and provided with proper food and shelter.

Nagam has also implemented strategies to control the stray animal population through humane methods, including sterilization and adoption programs. The goal is to reduce the number of strays and ensure a healthier, safer environment for both animals and citizens.

Public awareness campaigns are regularly conducted to educate citizens on how to report sightings of stray animals and what steps they can take to ensure animal welfare, such as not abandoning pets or supporting the animal control programs.
